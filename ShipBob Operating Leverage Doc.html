ShipBob Operating Leverage Doc


Our Contribution Margin % has grown from 9% in 2018 to 54% in April 2020. Where are the levers to grow our Contribution Margins ?


  • Contribution Margin Dollars are the dollars left behind after paying for all the variable and fixed costs of operating our fulfillment network .
    • It is a good proxy of long term EBITDA Margins as HQ costs below CM remain relatively fixed and as net revenue grows quickly, HQ costs become a smaller % of net revenue
  • A growing Contribution Margin % implies growing leverage in the business .

First the definitions :


  • The 3 big levers for growing Contribution Margin % :
  1. Net Revenue expands as our shipping costs go down with scale.
  2. FC Labor Costs as a % of Net Revenue go down
  • This is measured by VCPO - Variable Costs per Order
  1. FC Costs being a fixed cost remain fixed while the net revenue grows.



Lets walk through it :

  1. Net Revenue Expansion
  • This is simply a function of scale as we are able to negotiate volume discounts from shipping carriers with our growing scale. Our fulfillment prices to our merchants remain fixed but our shipping costs go down. Hence the expansion of the net revenue %.
  • In 2018 48% of our gross revenue was spent on shipping costs.
  • A year later and having doubled in scale, our shipping costs went down 4 points to 44% of revenue in 2019.
  • In Q1 2020 - our shipping costs were flat to 2019 - 1 point better than what we have projected for 2020.
    • April was higher as carriers implemented COVID surcharges that we decided not not to pass through to our merchants during these times.
  • Going forward while we continue to double our shipping volumes, we anticipate an extremely conservative 1 point of improvement on shipping costs every go forward year.



  1. Direct FC Labor Costs as a % of Net Revenue
  • We are able to make consistent improvements in the Warehouse Operating Software and the physical processes inside the FC .
    • This increases the throughput of our FC operations as measured by the OPH metric ( Orders per hour)
    • This in-turn improves our VCPO metric (Variable Costs per Order ) which is a measure of our labor efficiency ( how much in labor $$ does it cost us to ship an order out)
    • As our FC employees are getting more productive, our labor costs as a % of net revenue goes down. Hence the advantage of owning full stack !

  • By our OPH/ VCPO improvements, our labor costs as a % of net revenue went down 7 points from 36% in 2018 to 29% in 2019.
  • In 2020 Q1 our VCPO was $1.37 - an improvement of 18% over Q4 2019. This means every order that we have shipped in Q1 2020 cost us 18% less in labor costs compared to 2019 !
    • In April 2020 - our labor spend is at 21 % of net revenue which is close to what we have projected for 2022 !!
    • This is because we were able to get more densities in our pick paths and our Warehouse Operating System was able to better allocate processing paths.



  1. Fulfillment Center Rent and Facility Costs -
  • This includes our FC rent costs alongside FC management salaries ( General Managers, Operations Managers) , FC utility-bills, FC workers comp. insurance costs etc - everything needed to keep the FC going.
  • At the end of 2019 - we only were 20% utilized in our current FC network. So we have plenty of capacity in our network to keep adding new merchants and grow revenue. See here for capacity
  • For every new client that we bring onboard to ShipBob - we don’t open another Fulfillment center ( no increase in rent) or hire another General Manager / Operations Manager to run our Fulfillment Centers.

  • So our fixed expenses for renting and managing our fulfillment centers tend to remain fairly flat (Orange Line) but we are able to drive more net revenue through the FCs (Blue Line) - Operational Efficiency at work!. Hence on a % basis our FC costs continue coming down. ShipBob is a fixed costs investment business. You make this initial investment and let the revenue dollars pay for the fixed costs over a period of time.


  • On our SFN (partner facilities) since we don’t operate ( no rent ) or run the day to day management of these locations ( no General Managers or Area Managers) - our FC costs on the partner network are negligible.
  • Our FC costs as a % of net revenue went down 21 points from 55% in 2018 to 34% in Q1 2020.
  • In April 2020 , our FC costs as a % of net revenue was down another 11 points to 24%.
  • We will be steady state closer to 12% - which also includes the rent and operations expense of adding an additional FC towards the end of 2021/early 2022.


The net result of :

  1. Shipping Efficiencies
  2. Labor Efficiencies
  3. FC Fixed Costs remaining fixed

  • Our Contribution Margins have grown from 9% in 2018 to 30% in 2019 to 54% in April 2020 and steady state to 71%.

If you want to pair this with EBITDA :

  • EBITDA $= CM $ - HQ Costs $
  • EBITDA Margin %= (EBITDA $ / NetRevenue $) *100



  • Our EBITDA margins are 41% - more than double compared to a typical 3PL while still investing in growth aggressively.
