Operating Leverage

Our Contribution Margins have grown from 4% in 2018 to 16% in 2019 to 27% in April 2020 and we think steady state closer to 37%. Where are the levers to grow our Contribution Margins ?


First the definitions :

  • The 3 big levers for growing Contribution Margin % :
  1. Shipping Costs as a % of Revenue go down.
  2. Direct FC Labor Costs as a % of Revenue go down
  • This is measured by VCPO - Variable Costs per Order
  1. FC Costs being a fixed cost remain fixed while we keep increasing the revenue flowing through the network.


Lets walk through it :

  1. Shipping Costs as a % of Revenue :
  • This is simply a function of scale as we get volume discounts from shipping carriers as our revenue goes up but our pricing to our merchants remains un-changed.
  • From 2018 to 2019 - Our shipping costs went down 3 points from 51% of revenue in 2018 to 48% of revenue in 2019.
  • In Q1 2020 - our shipping costs were 47% of revenue - 1 point of improvement over 2019.
  • Going forward : We anticipate a very conservative 1 point of improvement for every go forward year.


  1. Direct FC Labor Costs as a % of Revenue
  • We are able to make consistent improvements in the Warehouse Operating Software and the physical processes inside the FC .
    • This increases the throughput of our FC operations as measured by the OPH metric ( Orders per hour)
    • This in-turn improves our VCPO metric (Variable Costs per Order ) which is a measure of our labor efficiency ( how much in labor $$ does it cost us to ship an order out)

  • As our FC employees are getting more productive, our labor costs as a % of revenue goes down. Hence the advantage of owning full stack !
    • By our VCPO improvements, our labor costs as a % of revenue went down 3 points from 18% in 2018 to 15% in 2019.
  • In 2020 Q1 our VCPO is closer to $1.37 - an improvement of 18% over Q4 2019. This means every order that we have shipped in Q1 2020 cost us 18% less in labor costs compared to 2019 !
  • In April 2020 - we are at 11 % of revenue which is better than what we have projected for 2024 !!


  1. Fulfillment Center Rent and Facility Costs -
  • This includes our FC rent costs alongside FC management salaries ( General Managers, Operations Managers) , FC utility-bills, FC workers comp. insurance costs etc - everything needed to keep the FC going.
  • At the end of 2019 - we only were 20% utilized in our current FC network. So we have plenty of capacity in our network to keep adding new merchants and grow revenue. See here for capacity
  • For every new client that we bring onboard to ShipBob - we don’t open another Fulfillment center ( no increase in rent) or hire another General Manager / Operations Manager to run our Fulfillment Centers.

  • So our fixed expenses for renting and managing our fulfillment centers tend to be fairly flat (Orange Line) but we are able to drive more revenue through the FCs (Blue Line) - Operational Efficiency at work!


  • Our FC costs as a % of revenue went down 6 points from 27% in 2018 to 21% in 2019.
  • In April 2020 , our FC costs as a % of revenue went down a further 9 points to 12%.
  • We think we will be steady state close to 7% - which includes the rent and operations expense of an additional FC towards the end of 2021/early 2022.


The net result of :

  1. Shipping Efficiencies
  2. Labor Efficiencies
  3. Fixed Costs remaining fixed

  • Our Contribution Margins have grown from 4% in 2018 to 16% in 2019 to 27% in April 2020 and steady state closer to 37%.

If you want to pair this with EBITDA :

EBITDA = CM $$ - HQ Costs

  • Our steady state EBITDA margins are closer to 20%
