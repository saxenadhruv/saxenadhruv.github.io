How has ShipBob benefited from being a full stack solution?


The advantages of being full stack are reflected in 4 key areas:

  1. Acquisition, Retention and expanding our merchants by offering new products and capabilities
  2. Growing Operational Capabilities and Operating Leverage
  3. Merchant Experience

All of this results in better Gross Margins/Contribution margins than a standard 3PL.

Acquisition

  1. We are able to service and bring on-board thousands of merchants as compared to a traditional 3PL who only has the capacity to bring in 6-12 merchants in a year.
  • Our on-boarding time for a merchant is less than a day as compared to a typical 3PL which takes months to on-board as they build the software and the physical infrastructure to service the new merchant.
  1. Growth Program has no minimums : Since our incremental cost of bringing a merchant on our network is 0 ( because of automated on-boarding and setup- no human required !) - We are able to cater to the SMB D2C space which has been very underserved. Most 3PLs have high minimum shipment volumes (1000 shipments/month) as they don’t want to hire and build the infrastructure if the brand is not generating enough revenue.

Some proof points :

  • Our 3PL network partner in Carrollton, TX operating independently for the past 15 years has 60 of their own merchants. Just in the last 2 months - ShipBob has on-boarded 108 new merchants for that same network partner who is using our software and our FC blueprint to service our merchant base.
  • For Q1 2020 , across all our sales channels - we on-boarded 253 new merchants. 95 merchants came through growth ( no human touch) .
    • These 253 merchants in-aggregate generated $1.8M of new shipping revenue in Q1 2020 itself , considering anyone who started in Feb and March did not have a full quarter !


Retention

  • Inventory : How much inventory of a product do I have in stock at a FC, Can I send you more inventory to your particular FC
  • Sales Channels : How do I connect more sales channels like Amazon, Walmart to ShipBob, can I add some marketing collateral to my order, Can I create virtual bundles of products on my e-commerce store without making any supply chain changes.

  1. Consistency of experience : The reason why 3PLs don’t want to work with SMB brands at scale is it’s difficult to provide consistency of experience at scale as decisions are made by FC employees which is not scalable. For example - The same order can be packaged by the packers in different boxes depending on their best judgement. This impacts merchant pricing. Using our full stack approach - we solved the consistency of packaging and pricing across thousands of merchants where the decision is made by Warehouse Operating System instead of the FC associates.

Operational Leverage

Logistics is a at-scale business. You make fixed costs investments upfront and then let scale do its work.

  1. By owning the WOS and the FCs and by making consistent improvements in both layers of the stack - it increases the throughput of our FC operations which reduces our variable operating expenses. These savings flow up-steam - creating a better product for our merchants which then join our network - bringing further scale which then further improves our throughput .

Proof points :

  • VCPO ( Variable cost per order) - How much in labor does it cost us to ship every order out.

As we get larger and our processes and software gets better - we should be able to show operational efficiencies by reducing our VCPO. This means every year, our fulfillment center associates should be able to pick, pack and ship more orders per hour than they did in the year prior.



In 2020 Q1 our VCPO is closer to $1.37 - an improvement of 18% over Q4 2019. This means every order that we have shipped in Q1 2020 cost us 18% less in labor costs compared to 2019 ! This is operational efficiency at work!


  • Fulfillment Center Rent and Facility Costs - How much does it cost us to rent and keep the lights on at our Fulfillment centers. So this includes our FC rent alongside FC management salaries , FC utilities, FC insurance costs etc - everything needed to keep the FC going.

    • For every new client that we bring onboard to ShipBob :: we don’t open another Fulfillment center ( no increase in rent) or hire another General Manager / Operations Manager to run our Fulfillment Centers.
